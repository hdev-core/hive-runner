# Hive Runner — Story Bible: *Keepers of the Chain*

The narrative frame for Hive Runner. Its job is to make **every existing mechanic mean something** —
nothing here changes gameplay; it gives the gameplay a *reason*. Tone: earnest, hopeful, a little punk.
Decentralization as quiet heroism — never grimdark, never preachy.

---

## Logline

> Every three seconds the Hive seals a new block. No server carries it onward — only the **Couriers**.
> You are one of them: run each block down the chain to the next Witness before it fades, and prove the
> world doesn't need a master to keep its memory.

---

## The world

**The Hive** is a living, decentralized world written one block at a time. There is no central keeper —
the world holds together because its people (Hivers) keep *feeding* it: posting, voting, moving, living.

Every **~3 seconds** (real Hive block time — it's why blocks stream past you) the Hive seals a new
**block**: a parcel of everyone's stories, votes, and memories. A sealed block is fragile. If it isn't
carried onward and confirmed by a **Witness**, it slips into **the Fade** — and that slice of history is
lost forever. The chain only exists because someone keeps running.

That someone is a **Courier**.

## You

A Courier of the Hive — a runner of the **Guild of Keepers**. You've just earned your parcel-pack (the
Hive-branded box on your back). Your strength isn't issued to you; it comes from your standing in the
Hive itself. **The more alive you are on-chain — posting, voting, and moving in the real world — the
stronger you run.** A quiet Hiver runs light; an active one runs like the wind.

## The conflict

**The Monolith** — a single, enormous, centralized machine — has offered to "fix" the Hive: retire the
messy, scattered couriers and let one perfect server carry every block. Faster, it says. Cleaner. Safer.
It just wants the keys to everyone's history.

The Monolith can't be fought head-on. It's fought one delivery at a time. Every block a Courier runs to a
Witness is a small, stubborn proof that people — plural, decentralized, imperfect — can keep their own
memory alive. The threats you dodge are the Monolith's world leaking in: **congestion and stalled
transactions** (the block hazards you leap), and the creeping quiet of the **Fade** at your heels.

## The Guild & rivals

Couriers run together. Your **ghost rivals** are fellow Keepers — your Hive follows and community — on
parallel deliveries. It's a rivalry, not a war: you race to pass them, and passing your own past-best
self (your ghost) is the truest race of all. The **weekly contest** is the Guild's **Delivery League**.

## Glossary — mechanic → meaning

| What you see (already in the game) | What it *is* in the story |
|---|---|
| The parcel on your back | A freshly-sealed block, mid-delivery |
| Blocks streaming past; `#num · @witness` coins | The live chain; Witnesses are the waypoint stations you hand off to |
| Gold coins / pickups | Resource Credits & upvotes — the fuel that keeps you moving |
| Block **hazards** you jump | Congestion — stalled/stuck transactions clogging the lane |
| **Hearts** | An upvote from the community — a little love that revives you |
| **Ghost racers** | Fellow Couriers (your follows / community / past self) |
| **Energy** (RC/mana, 24h ops, Actifit steps) | Your standing in the Hive — active Hivers run stronger; your real steps are your stamina |
| **Trails / skins / themes** | A Courier's colors — Guild pride, earned on the road |
| **Free-play perks** | Off-the-record training gear; barred from League runs so the League stays fair |
| Faster, busier levels | Running **deeper into the chain** — block production never sleeps |
| **Weekly contest** | The Guild's **Delivery League** |

## The level arc (surfaced as level names)

The run reads as a descent *deeper into the chain*, each stretch busier and faster:

1. **The Mempool** — *unconfirmed; keep it moving*
2. **Witness Row** — *hand off, don't drop it*
3. **The Fast Lane** — *the blocks are tightening*
4. **Consensus Gorge** — *faster · busier*
5. **The Deep Chain** — *no machine can follow you here*
6. **The Long Fork** — *hold the line*

(Endless: the names cycle, each loop "deeper." Level-complete = **"Block delivered!"** — a Witness
handoff.)

## How it surfaces in-game (shipped)

- **Intro card** — a skippable opening beat on first visit; replayable via **📖 Story**.
- **Level names + banners** — "The Mempool → Witness Row → …"; level-complete reads **"Block delivered!"**.
- **Tagline + hint copy** — reframed around the delivery premise.
- **Tooltips** — the energy chip already explains itself; copy leans into "your standing powers your run."
